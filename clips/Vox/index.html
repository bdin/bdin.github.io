Why you should hire me, explained

Updated by Benjamin Din on November 13, 2015, 8:40 AM ET@benjamindin benjamin.din@gmail.com

Photo by Kelsey Pukelis

Benjamin Din, explained

As a student journalist at Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism, I've gotten involved in a variety of journalism-related activites, namely The Daily Northwestern and the Knight Lab. At The Daily, I have learned and developed the reporting and writing skills that would make successful at Vox. From copy editing articles to publishing and promoting them on social media to designing pages for our print newspaper and our website, I've been involved in a variety of roles at the only indepent student-run newspaper on campus. At the Knight Lab, I'm currently developing my web programming skills in Django and Python, with a hope of dabbling in SQL before the end of the academic year.

However, what makes me the best candidate for an internship at Vox are the other subjects I'm studying at NU: the mathematical methods in the social sciences and Asian-American studies. As an MMSS double major, I'm learning how math and economics can intersect in a variety of fields that one would not expect. This quarter, I'm taking a statistics course – definitely the hardest class I've taken here – and I'm learning so many things related to data analysis. In my Asian-American studies classes, I'm learning how race and identity play a role in our society. Given recent events, there's nothing more important to focus on in my opinion. Race and identity are major issues that need to be written on, and written on by people who are informed. When the Vox published "3 reasons the American Revolution was a mistake," I learned that Vox is not an organization afraid to tackle these issues. As a journalist who is interested in tackling hard issues, I'm excited for the opportunity to work this summer at Vox.
